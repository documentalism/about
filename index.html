About

I am an award-winning documentary Producer/Director with over 15 years’ experience delivering high-quality factual films across conservation, science and international development contexts. I have led and shot major international productions for broadcasters including the BBC, National Geographic and Apple TV+, with a strong focus on environmental storytelling, human–nature relationships and translating complex scientific work into clear, audience-focused narratives. My work spans both long-form documentary and multi-platform content, enabling organisations to communicate impact across broadcast, digital. I bring substantial field experience across Africa and other remote regions, working in logistically complex and environmentally sensitive contexts. Recent and relevant credits include conservation and wildlife productions in Botswana and Gabon, science-led expedition filmmaking, and directing projects across East and West Africa that required close collaboration with local contributors, authorities and production partners. Alongside broadcast work, I have delivered communications films and visual storytelling projects for Geneva-based humanitarian and policy organisations, combining strong editorial judgement with practical, low-footprint production methods designed for remote, low-impact fieldwork. This blend of broadcast-level craft, conservation storytelling experience and operational reliability in remote settings positions me to deliver consistent, high-quality documentary outputs across the East and Southern Africa region.
